## 1. Iranian Strike Package Composition

The IRGC strike package was structured in three waves, separated by approximately 25-minute intervals to saturate Israeli air defences through sequential engagement rather than simultaneous overload.

**Wave 1 (02:15–02:35 UTC):** 147 Shahed-136 one-way attack drones launched from three sites. The Shahed-136 is a delta-wing loitering munition with a range of approximately 2,500 kilometres, carrying a 40-kilogram warhead. Cruise speed is approximately 185 km/h, making it the slowest element of the strike package and the first to be detected. Transit time from the Kermanshah launch sites to the Negev targets was approximately 9 hours, meaning the drones were launched well before the ballistic missiles, though this was not immediately apparent to observers.

**Wave 2 (02:40–02:55 UTC):** 32 Shahab-3 medium-range ballistic missiles launched from hardened silos in western Iran. The Shahab-3 has a range of 1,300 to 2,000 kilometres depending on variant, with a payload capacity of approximately 760 kilograms. Four of the 32 were identified as the extended-range variant (Shahab-3B/Ghadr-1) with a range exceeding 1,800 kilometres, which followed higher-apogee trajectories and were engaged by the Arrow-3 system at exoatmospheric altitudes.

**Wave 3 (03:00–03:15 UTC):** 18 Soumar ground-launched cruise missiles fired from positions in eastern Syria. The Soumar is based on the Soviet Kh-55 design, with an estimated range of 2,500 kilometres and terrain-following capability. Its low-altitude flight profile (50–100 metres above ground level) makes it more difficult to detect than ballistic missiles but slower than ballistic trajectories.

## 2. Israeli Layered Air Defence Performance

Israel's multi-layered air defence architecture was engaged across all tiers simultaneously — the first time all four layers have been activated in a single engagement.

**Iron Dome:** Engaged primarily against Shahed-136 drones and Soumar cruise missiles. The system fired approximately 210 Tamir interceptors against 147 drones and 18 cruise missiles (165 targets). Confirmed intercepts: 94 drones (64% of drones engaged) and 14 cruise missiles (78% of cruise missiles engaged). The lower interception rate against drones compared to the system's typical 90%+ success rate against rockets is attributed to the Shahed-136's small radar cross-section and the sheer volume of simultaneous targets.

**David's Sling:** Engaged against Shahab-3 ballistic missiles at medium altitude. The system fired 34 Stunner interceptors against 28 of the 32 incoming ballistic missiles (the remaining 4 were assigned to Arrow-3 due to their higher trajectories). Confirmed intercepts: 28 of 28 targets engaged — a 100% success rate for the missiles assigned to this tier. David's Sling's hit-to-kill kinetic warhead performed as designed against the relatively predictable Shahab-3 trajectory.

**Arrow-3:** Engaged 4 high-apogee Shahab-3B/Ghadr-1 variants at exoatmospheric altitudes exceeding 100 kilometres. All 4 were intercepted. Arrow-3's exoatmospheric intercept capability — destroying warheads in space before re-entry — is considered the most technically demanding tier and performed flawlessly.

**IAF F-35I Adir:** Four F-35I aircraft scrambled from Nevatim Air Base engaged Soumar cruise missiles using AIM-120D AMRAAM air-to-air missiles. At least 4 cruise missiles were intercepted by F-35I aircraft, though the exact number attributed to air-to-air versus Iron Dome is not publicly confirmed.

## 3. US Naval Intercept Operations

Two Arleigh Burke-class destroyers — USS Thomas Hudner (DDG-116) and USS Ramage (DDG-61) — participated in the intercept operation from positions in the Eastern Mediterranean. Both ships are equipped with the Aegis Baseline 9 combat system and carry SM-3 Block IIA interceptors designed for ballistic missile defence.

CENTCOM confirmed engagement of "at least 6" incoming ballistic missiles by US naval assets. The SM-3 Block IIA, co-developed by the US and Japan, is a hit-to-kill interceptor capable of engaging medium- and intermediate-range ballistic missiles at altitudes up to 1,000 kilometres. The successful engagement of Iranian ballistic missiles by US destroyers marks the second operational use of SM-3 against Iranian projectiles, following the April 2024 precedent.

The USS Gerald R. Ford carrier strike group repositioned from the central Mediterranean to a position approximately 180 nautical miles west of the Israeli coast within 12 hours of the strike. The Ford's air wing (Carrier Air Wing 8) includes F/A-18E/F Super Hornets, EA-18G Growler electronic warfare aircraft, and E-2D Advanced Hawkeye airborne early warning aircraft. No offensive operations were conducted by the carrier air wing.

## 4. Damage Assessment (Military)

Post-strike satellite imagery analysis confirms the following damage:

**Sdot Micha Air Base:** Two impact points on the western perimeter. A maintenance hangar sustained structural damage (roof collapse on the eastern section). A taxiway connector between the main runway and the hardened aircraft shelters was cratered, requiring approximately 18 hours to repair. No aircraft shelters were breached. No combat aircraft were on the damaged taxiway at the time of impact.

**Ramon Airfield:** Three Shahed-136 impacts within the airfield boundary. Two ground support vehicles (a fuel bowser and a mobile generator unit) were destroyed. A TPS-77 Multi-Role Radar installation sustained damage to its antenna array, temporarily degrading radar coverage in the southern sector. The radar was replaced by a mobile unit within 12 hours.

**Tze'elim Training Complex:** Six drone impacts in open desert terrain within the complex boundary. No structural targets were hit. Secondary scrubland fires burned approximately 4 hectares before being contained by IDF fire teams.

## 5. Iranian Operational Assessment

From a military-operational perspective, the Iranian strike achieved limited tactical results relative to the resources expended. The cost of the strike package — estimated at $85–120 million based on publicly available production cost estimates for Shahed-136 ($20,000–50,000 each), Shahab-3 ($1.5–3 million each), and Soumar ($3–5 million each) — far exceeded the value of the infrastructure damaged.

However, the strike served several strategic purposes. It demonstrated Iran's ability to conduct a multi-domain, multi-vector strike across multiple countries simultaneously. It tested Israeli air defences at scale, generating valuable telemetry data on Iron Dome, David's Sling, and Arrow-3 engagement patterns. And it established a deterrence precedent: Iran demonstrated willingness to conduct direct strikes against Israeli territory in response to targeted assassinations.

## 6. Force Posture Post-Strike

Israel mobilised two reserve infantry brigades (approximately 6,000 personnel) and deployed three additional Iron Dome batteries to northern Israel — two near Haifa and one near Kiryat Shmona. This northern redeployment suggests IDF concern about potential Hezbollah escalation, though no Hezbollah action materialised within the 72-hour observation window.

The US raised force protection conditions at all installations in CENTCOM's area of responsibility to FPCON Charlie. Additional Patriot PAC-3 batteries were moved to Al Udeid Air Base (Qatar) and Al Dhafra Air Base (UAE) as a precautionary measure. The B-1B Lancer bomber task force at Diego Garcia was placed on heightened alert.
